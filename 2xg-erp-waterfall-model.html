Procurement Process How goods are purchased and stocked

1

Register a Vendor

Add a new supplier with their contact person, phone, email, city, state, country, and payment terms (e.g. "Net 30" or "Due on Receipt").

Admin / Purchase Manager
2

Create a Purchase Order (PO)

Select the vendor, add items with quantities and agreed prices. The PO is a formal request to buy goods. It can be saved as draft first and sent to the vendor later.

Purchase Manager
3

Receive Goods & Create Bill

When goods arrive, convert the PO into a Bill. Each bill line item records the exact quantity received, cost price, serial numbers (if tracked), and any TDS/TCS tax deductions.

Warehouse / Accounts
4

Allocate Stock to Bins

For each item on the bill, choose which warehouse bin to store it in (e.g. "A-01-01", "BIN-003"). The system records a bin allocation ‚Äî this is how stock enters the warehouse.

Example: 100 units of "Widget X" received ‚Üí 60 placed in BIN-001 (Ground Floor), 40 placed in BIN-015 (1st Floor).
Warehouse Staff
5

Record Payment to Vendor

Create a "Payment Made" record against the bill. Track how much was paid, the payment method (cash, bank, UPI), and any remaining balance. If the vendor overcharged, issue a Vendor Credit.

Accounts
‚úì

Result

Stock is now in the warehouse bins. The vendor ledger shows the bill and payment. Inventory counts are updated. The items are ready to sell.

Sales Process How goods are sold and delivered to customers

1

Register a Customer

Add customer name, company, email, phone, billing address, shipping address, GSTIN/PAN (for tax), and payment terms.

Sales Team
2

Create a Sales Order (SO)

Customer requests items. Create a sales order listing what they want, quantities, and selling prices. This is a commitment to sell ‚Äî no stock moves yet.

Sales Team
3

Generate Invoice

Convert the sales order into an invoice. The invoice is the official billing document with tax calculations (TDS/TCS), totals, and due dates. This is what the customer pays against.

Accounts / Sales
4

Pick Stock from Bins

For each item on the invoice, select which bin to take stock from. The system creates invoice bin allocations ‚Äî this is how stock leaves the warehouse.

Example: Customer ordered 25 units of "Widget X" ‚Üí Pick 25 from BIN-001 (Ground Floor). BIN-001 stock drops from 60 to 35.
Warehouse Staff
5

Create Delivery Challan & Dispatch

Generate a delivery challan ‚Äî a document that travels with the goods. It lists items, quantities, and the destination. Hand over to logistics for delivery.

Logistics
6

Receive Payment from Customer

Record the payment received against the invoice ‚Äî full or partial. Track payment method and any outstanding balance.

Accounts
‚úì

Result

Goods delivered to customer. Payment collected. Stock reduced from the correct bins. Customer ledger updated. Revenue recorded in reports.

Point of Sale (POS) Walk-in counter sales ‚Äî quick checkout

1

Open a POS Session

A cashier opens a new session for the day. This tracks all sales, payments, and cash movements during that session. The opening cash balance is recorded.

Cashier
2

Add Items to Cart

Scan barcodes or search items. Select the bin to pick from (system shows available stock per bin). Add quantities and the selling price is auto-filled.

Cashier
3

Collect Payment

Customer pays. Supports split payment ‚Äî for example ‚Çπ500 cash + ‚Çπ1,200 UPI. Each portion is recorded separately with its payment method.

Cashier
4

Auto-Generated Invoice & Stock Update

The system automatically creates an invoice, a "payment received" record, and deducts stock from the selected bins. No manual steps needed.

System (Automatic)
5

Close Session & Reconcile

At end of day, close the session. The system shows total sales, total cash collected, and payment breakdowns. Cashier counts physical cash and records the closing balance.

Cashier / Manager
‚úì

Result

All walk-in sales recorded. Stock automatically reduced. Cash balanced. Session report available for management review.

Warehouse & Stock Management How stock is organized, moved, and counted

1

Set Up Locations & Bins

Define physical locations (e.g. "Warehouse A", "Head Office", "Shop Floor"). Within each location, create bin codes (e.g. "A-01-01", "BIN-003") to track exactly where items sit.

Warehouse Manager
2

Stock Comes In (via Bills)

When a purchase bill is created, stock is allocated to specific bins. This is the only way stock enters the system. Every unit is traceable to a bin.

Warehouse Staff
3

Transfer Stock Between Locations

Need to move items from one location to another? Create a Transfer Order. Select source bin, destination bin, and quantity. When the order is "initiated", the stock moves. If cancelled, stock reverts.

Draft ‚Üí no movement
Initiated ‚Üí stock deducted from source bin, added to destination bin
Received ‚Üí transfer confirmed
Cancelled ‚Üí movement reversed
Warehouse Manager
4

Conduct Stock Counts

Periodically count physical stock and compare with system records. Mobile app staff can scan items and record counts. Discrepancies are flagged for investigation.

Warehouse Staff / Mobile Users
5

Report Damaged Stock

If items are found damaged, create a damage report with type, severity, and quantity. This helps management track losses and decide on write-offs or insurance claims.

Warehouse Staff
6

Stock Goes Out (via Invoices / POS)

When a sale is made, stock is deducted from the selected bins via invoice or POS bin allocations. The cycle completes ‚Äî goods received, stored, managed, and sold.

System (Automatic)
‚úì

Stock Formula

Stock in any bin = Purchases In ‚àí Sales Out ‚àí Transfers Out + Transfers In. The system calculates this in real-time. No manual stock registers needed.
